<p><p>Wood gypsum composites are a type of construction material that combines wood fibers with gypsum to create a strong and lightweight building material. There are two main types of wood gypsum composites: monolayer wood gypsum composites and multi-storey wood gypsum composites. </p><p>Monolayer wood gypsum composites consist of a single layer of wood fibers mixed with gypsum. These composites are commonly used in applications where a lightweight and fire-resistant material is needed, such as interior wall panels.</p><p>Multi-storey wood gypsum composites, on the other hand, are designed for use in multi-storey buildings. These composites feature multiple layers of wood fibers and gypsum, providing increased strength and stability. They are often used in the construction of floors, walls, and roofs in high-rise buildings.</p></p>
